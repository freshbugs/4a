Clarification of Theorem 6

Suppose you have a non-homogeneous system of linear equations that has infinitely many solutions, and the "corresponding homogeneous system" is the same but with all zeros on the right of the equals signs.

Let's say Ns are solutions to the non-homogeneous system, and Hs are solutions to the corresponding homogeneous system.

An N plus an H is always another N.

Compare, the superposition principle says an H plus an H is always another H.

Leontief exchange model

Alice, Bob and Charlie live on an island. The economy consists of three products: apples, bananas and cherimoyas.

  • Alice sells one apple to Bob and one to Charlie.
  • Bob sells two bananas to Charlie.
  • Charlie sells one cherimoya to Alice.

Question: If everybody ends up with the same amount of money they started with, which fruit is the most expensive?

The prices are $a$, $b$ and $c$. Alice receives and gives the same amount of money. Same for Bob and Charlie.

  • $2a = c$
  • $2b = a$
  • $c = a + 2b$

Solve. There is one free variable. Check your answer.

Answer: Cherimoyas. (Or maybe the trivial solution: all fruits are free.)

Leontief did something like this in 1949 using real-world data and a computer.

Balancing chemical equations

When methane burns, it combines with oxygen to form carbon dioxide and water.

⬜ CH4 + ⬜ O2   ⟶   ⬜ CO2 + ⬜ H2O

Question: How many O2 molecules does it take to burn each CH4 molecule?

Let the blanks be $x$, $y$, $z$ and $w$. To balance the carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen atoms:

  • $x = z$
  • $4x = 2w$
  • $2y = 2z + w$

Solve. There is one free variable. Check your answer.

Answer: Two.

Network flow

A traffic circle connects four roads. Every minute:

  • One car enters from the west.
  • One car enters from the east.
  • One car leaves to the north.
  • One car leaves to the south.

Question: Which quarter(s) of the circle get the most traffic?

Let $a$, $b$, $c$, $d$ be the number of cars per minute on the north-west, north-east, south-west and south-east quarter circles.

The north point on the circle has the same number of cars entering and leaving. Same for west, east, and south.

  • $b = a + 1$
  • $a + 1 = c$
  • $d + 1 = b$
  • $c = d + 1$

Solve. There is one free variable. Check your answer.

Answer: The north-east and south-west quarters of the circle both get the most traffic.

Bonus: What is the solution to the corresponding homogeneous system? (Hint: the roundabout in the movie "European vacation").

The same model applies whenever stuff is moving through a network — electrons through circuits, products through distribution networks, water through pipes, etc.

The original Google Page Rank Algorithm (TM) uses linear algebra to decide which web pages are the most important. It's a bit like traffic flow where the "cars" are web surfers clicking links at random.

Curve fitting

Question: A parabola goes through $(1, 3)$, $(2, 7)$ and $(-1, 1)$. What is the $y$-intercept?

$y = ax^2 + bx + c$. To go through those three points:

  • $a + b + c = 3$
  • $4a + 2b + c = 7$
  • $a - b + c = 1$

Solve. There is a unique solution. Check your answer.

Answer: $1$

(The parabola is also called an interpolating polynomial. See the exercises at the end of §1.2.)
